How AI helped us build the 2026 Workplace Point of View

A behind-the-scenes look at the human–AI collaboration process that shaped this year's WX POV — from raw thinking to structured strategy.

YS
Yester Sabondzhyan Sr. Director, CBRE Americas Consulting — Workplace  |  Los Angeles
The Premise

Strategy starts with conversation.

The 2026 WX POV didn't begin with a blank deck. It began with a dialogue — between a senior strategist and an AI collaborator. Here's how we worked through it, step by step.

1
Context Setting

I introduced myself and the project

The session opened with me sharing who I am, where I sit within CBRE, and what we were trying to build — a forward-looking Workplace POV document to frame CBRE's position heading into 2026.

"Sr. Director, CBRE Americas Consulting, Workplace team — based in Los Angeles. We're building a 2026 WX POV. Four themes. Needs to ladder back to CBRE methodology and brand."

2
Brand System Upload

I shared the CBRE brand and methodology DNA

Rather than working from scratch, I uploaded the Q1 2026 Proposal Template and Workplace Policy document directly into the project. Claude extracted the brand system — colors, typography, layout conventions — and the CBRE methodology framework.

Extracted: Deep forest green #004438, bright green #00B04F, off-white #F5F5F5 — plus serif/sans type pairing, split layouts, green rule conventions, and the CBRE WX Venn diagram framework.

Methodology captured: The Why / The Who / The What / The How — Research → Strategy → Recommendations process — 300K+ employees surveyed, 11K C-suite leaders interviewed.

3
POV Theme Development

We brought the whiteboard session — and refined the four buckets together

I shared the output from a live team whiteboard session — four potential theme buckets we'd already identified. Claude helped us work through the language, tighten the logic, and fine-tune each theme until they held together as a coherent point of view rather than a loose collection of ideas.

Starting point: four rough buckets from the whiteboard. End point: four distinct, strategically grounded themes — each with its own claim, internal logic, and connection to CBRE's methodology.

4
Slide Architecture

We agreed on a slide structure

We evaluated two structural options and landed on Option B — a consistent four-part format per POV slide: theme headline, why it matters now, CBRE methodology connection, and a visual element drawn from CBRE's existing framework language.

Structure per slide: POV Theme Headline → Supporting context / why now → CBRE methodology connection → Visual from CBRE framework language.

5
Session Summary & Knowledge Base

I captured the session as a structured brief

At the close of the working session, I wrote a full session summary and uploaded it to the Claude Project. This ensures continuity — every future session in this project starts from an informed baseline, not zero.

The Output — 4 POV Themes

The themes we landed on.

Each theme is a strategic claim about the direction of workplace in 2026 — rooted in CBRE's research, methodology, and the lived experience of the workforce.

01

People are the point — everything else is infrastructure

A reframe for how organizations should think about their investments. Space, tech, and services exist to serve people — not the other way around. This theme anchors the entire POV.

02

Growth doesn't happen by accident

Career development, mentorship, and skill-building require intentional design. The workplace has a specific role to play in creating conditions where growth is visible, accessible, and expected.

03

Autonomy is the new attendance policy

The debate has shifted from where people work to how much control they have over their work experience. Trust and flexibility aren't perks — they're performance infrastructure.

04

The office just got a new job description

The office is no longer a default location. It's a purposeful destination — designed around the work that benefits most from presence: connection, collaboration, culture, and creative collision.

The AI wasn't the author.
I was. The AI was the thought partner.

Every strategic decision in this project — the themes, the structure, the brand — came from Yester. Claude's role was to listen deeply, reflect accurately, and help organize what was already there into something usable.
